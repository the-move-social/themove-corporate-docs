# Equity Incentive Plan

## The Move Social Inc.

*Adopted by the Board of Directors on _________, 2026*

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Equity Incentive Plan (the "Plan") is to attract, retain, and motivate employees, contractors, and advisors of The Move Social Inc. (the "Company") by providing them with the opportunity to acquire an equity interest in the Company.

---

## 2. Administration

### 2.1 Administrator

The Plan shall be administered by the Board of Directors of the Company (the "Administrator").

### 2.2 Authority

The Administrator shall have the authority to:

(a) Select eligible persons to receive awards;

(b) Determine the type, number, and terms of awards;

(c) Interpret the Plan and any award agreements;

(d) Establish rules and procedures for the Plan;

(e) Make all other determinations necessary or advisable for the administration of the Plan.

### 2.3 Decisions Final

All decisions of the Administrator shall be final, binding, and conclusive on all parties.

---

## 3. Shares Available

### 3.1 Share Reserve

The maximum number of shares of **Class B Common Stock** (non-voting) that may be issued under the Plan is **1,500,000 shares** (the "Share Reserve"). All shares issued under this Plan shall be Class B Common Stock, which carries no voting rights but has identical economic rights (dividends, distributions, liquidation proceeds) to Class A Common Stock on a per-share basis.

### 3.2 Shares Returned to Reserve

If any award granted under the Plan expires, is forfeited, or is terminated without having been exercised or settled in full, the shares subject to such award shall again become available for issuance under the Plan.

### 3.3 Adjustments

In the event of any stock split, stock dividend, recapitalization, reorganization, or similar change in the Company's capital structure, the Administrator shall make appropriate adjustments to the Share Reserve and to any outstanding awards.

---

## 4. Eligibility

### 4.1 Eligible Persons

Awards may be granted to:

(a) **Employees** of the Company (eligible for ISOs and NSOs)

(b) **Consultants and contractors** providing services to the Company (eligible for NSOs only)

(c) **Advisors** providing services to the Company (eligible for NSOs only)

### 4.2 ISOs

Incentive Stock Options ("ISOs") may only be granted to employees of the Company, as required by Section 422 of the Internal Revenue Code.

### 4.3 NSOs

Non-Qualified Stock Options ("NSOs") may be granted to any eligible person.

---

## 5. Stock Options

### 5.1 Grant

The Administrator may grant stock options to eligible persons. Each option grant shall be evidenced by a written Stock Option Grant Agreement specifying the terms of the award.

### 5.2 Exercise Price

The exercise price per share of each option shall be determined by the Administrator, but shall not be less than **100% of the fair market value** of a share of Class B Common Stock on the date of grant.

For ISOs granted to a person who owns more than 10% of the voting power of the Company, the exercise price shall not be less than **110% of fair market value**.

### 5.3 Fair Market Value

Fair market value shall be determined by the most recent 409A valuation obtained by the Company. **No options may be granted until the Company has obtained a 409A valuation.**

### 5.4 Vesting

Unless otherwise specified in the individual Stock Option Grant Agreement, options shall vest as follows:

- **25% of the shares** vest on the first anniversary of the vesting commencement date (the "Cliff")
- **The remaining 75%** vest in equal monthly installments over the following 36 months

The standard vesting period is four (4) years from the vesting commencement date.

### 5.5 Term

Each option shall expire no later than **ten (10) years** from the date of grant. For ISOs granted to a person who owns more than 10% of the voting power, the term shall not exceed **five (5) years**.

### 5.6 Exercise

Options may be exercised by delivering written notice to the Company specifying the number of shares to be purchased, together with payment of the exercise price. Payment may be made in:

(a) Cash or check;

(b) Such other methods as the Administrator may approve.

### 5.6A Early Exercise

The Administrator may, in its discretion, permit an optionee to exercise options prior to vesting ("Early Exercise"). Shares acquired through Early Exercise that have not yet vested ("Unvested Shares") shall be subject to:

(a) A Company repurchase right at the original exercise price paid per share, exercisable within ninety (90) days of the optionee's termination of service for any reason other than Cause;

(b) Upon termination for Cause, immediate forfeiture of all shares (vested and unvested) pursuant to Section 5.8;

(c) The restrictions and repurchase right shall lapse as shares vest according to the vesting schedule specified in the applicable Stock Option Grant Agreement.

The Company shall provide each optionee who elects Early Exercise with a pre-filled Section 83(b) election form and written filing instructions. The optionee shall deliver proof of 83(b) filing to the Company within thirty-five (35) days of exercise.

### 5.7 Termination of Service

Upon termination of the optionee's service relationship with the Company:

(a) **Voluntary resignation or termination without cause:** The optionee may exercise vested options for a period of **ninety (90) days** following termination.

(b) **Termination for cause:** All options (vested and unvested) shall terminate immediately.

(c) **Death or disability:** The optionee (or estate) may exercise vested options for a period of **twelve (12) months** following the event.

Unvested options shall terminate immediately upon termination of service, regardless of the reason for termination.

### 5.8 Definition of Cause

For purposes of this Plan, "Cause" means:

(a) Conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, a felony or any crime involving moral turpitude;

(b) Commission of fraud, embezzlement, or misappropriation against the Company;

(c) Willful and material breach of any written agreement with the Company;

(d) Willful failure to perform assigned duties for a period of thirty (30) days after written notice from the Board;

(e) Willful misconduct that is materially injurious to the Company.

---

## 6. Restricted Stock

### 6.1 Grant

The Administrator may grant restricted stock awards to eligible persons. Each restricted stock award shall be evidenced by a written agreement specifying the terms.

### 6.2 Restrictions

Shares of restricted stock shall be subject to such vesting conditions, forfeiture provisions, and transfer restrictions as the Administrator may determine.

---

## 7. General Provisions

### 7.1 Non-Transferability

Awards granted under the Plan may not be sold, transferred, pledged, assigned, or otherwise alienated, except by will or the laws of descent and distribution.

### 7.2 No Right to Continued Service

Nothing in the Plan or any award agreement shall confer upon any participant the right to continue in the service of the Company or affect the Company's right to terminate such service at any time.

### 7.3 Tax Withholding

The Company shall have the right to withhold from any payment or share issuance under the Plan any amounts necessary to satisfy applicable tax withholding obligations.

### 7.4 Securities Law Compliance

The Company shall not be required to issue shares under the Plan unless and until such issuance complies with all applicable federal and state securities laws.

### 7.5 No Stockholder Rights

An optionee shall have no rights as a stockholder with respect to shares subject to an option until such shares are issued upon exercise of the option.

### 7.6 Non-Voting Shares

All shares issued under this Plan are Class B Common Stock and carry no voting rights. Participants shall have no right to vote on any matter submitted to stockholders of the Company, except as may be required by applicable law.

---

## 8. Change of Control

### 8.1 Definition

A "Change of Control" means:

(a) A merger or consolidation in which the Company's stockholders immediately prior to the transaction hold less than 50% of the voting power of the surviving entity;

(b) A sale of all or substantially all of the Company's assets;

(c) Any person or group acquires more than 50% of the Company's outstanding voting securities.

### 8.2 Effect on Awards

In the event of a Change of Control, the Administrator may, in its discretion:

(a) Accelerate the vesting of all outstanding awards;

(b) Cancel outstanding awards in exchange for a cash payment equal to the difference between the exercise price and the per-share consideration in the transaction;

(c) Provide for the assumption or substitution of awards by the surviving entity;

(d) If the Administrator does not take action under (a), (b), or (c) within ten (10) days prior to the Change of Control, all outstanding awards shall automatically vest in full.

### 8.3 Floor Payout on Change of Control

In any Change of Control transaction, each holder of a vested option shall receive, at minimum, a cash payment equal to the positive difference (if any) between (i) the per-share consideration paid to stockholders in the transaction and (ii) the exercise price of the option, multiplied by the number of vested shares subject to the option (the "Floor Payment"). The Floor Payment shall be made at the closing of the Change of Control transaction and shall not be subject to or reduced by any liquidation preference, participation right, or other priority payment owed to holders of preferred stock or any other class of securities. If the acquiring entity does not agree to fund the Floor Payment, the Company shall reserve sufficient proceeds from the transaction to satisfy all Floor Payments before distributing any proceeds to preferred stockholders.

### 8.4 Pari Passu Treatment

Shares of Class B Common Stock issued or issuable upon exercise of options granted under this Plan shall be treated on a per-share basis identically to shares of Class A Common Stock for all purposes in connection with any Change of Control transaction, including but not limited to:

(a) Per-share merger consideration;

(b) Per-share distributions upon liquidation, dissolution, or winding up;

(c) Per-share payments in any asset sale.

No class of preferred stock, and no liquidation preference or participation right, shall reduce the per-share consideration payable to holders of Class B Common Stock below the per-share consideration payable to holders of Class A Common Stock. The Company shall not enter into any Change of Control transaction, and shall not agree to any terms with any investor or acquirer, that would result in holders of Class B Common Stock receiving less per share than holders of Class A Common Stock.

### 8.5 Covenant to Protect Option Holders

The Company covenants that in any future financing or issuance of preferred stock, the Company shall use commercially reasonable efforts to negotiate terms that preserve the rights of option holders under this Section 8, including but not limited to: (a) non-participating preferred stock structures; (b) carve-outs for common and option holders in any liquidation waterfall; and (c) caps on liquidation preferences that would otherwise eliminate payout to common stockholders.

---

## 9. Amendment and Termination

### 9.1 Amendment

The Administrator may amend the Plan at any time, provided that no amendment shall materially impair the rights of a participant under an outstanding award without the participant's consent.

### 9.2 Termination

The Plan shall terminate on the **tenth (10th) anniversary** of its adoption by the Board, unless terminated earlier by the Administrator. No awards may be granted after termination, but awards outstanding at termination shall continue in effect according to their terms.

---

## 10. Dispute Resolution

### 10.1 Mediation

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Plan, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall first be submitted to mandatory mediation. The parties shall select a mutually agreeable mediator within fifteen (15) days of either party's written request for mediation. The mediation shall be completed within thirty (30) days of the mediator's appointment.

### 10.2 Binding Arbitration

If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in the State of Delaware by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA rules. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

---

## 11. Governing Law

The Plan shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.
