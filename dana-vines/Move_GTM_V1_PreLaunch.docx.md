# **1\. What Is Move**

Move is an AI-powered social app that eliminates the friction of going out with your friends. You set the parameters \-- budget, duration, vibe \-- and Move builds a custom outing experience for your group in real time. No more endless group chats debating where to go. No more "wait, where are you?" texts. No more plans that die because planning got exhausting.

The app launches in NYC, where the options are infinite but the coordination overhead is just as infinite. Move solves that.

## **1.1 Core Features at Launch (V.1)**

| AI-Generated Outings | Users input budget, time available, and vibe. Move's AI returns a curated plan \-- venues, sequence, and timing \-- built for the group. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Live Position Tracking** | Every participant in a Move can see each other's real-time location. No more "I'm almost there" texts. Privacy-controlled and siloed to the active Move. |
| **Move Group Chat** | A dedicated, encrypted chat thread created for each Move. Keeps plans organized and main message threads clean. |
| **Vibe Parameters** | Users choose energy level, activity type, and spend range. The AI adapts. The experience feels curated, not random. |
| **Privacy by Design** | Location data, chat history, and movement patterns are encrypted. Move does not sell user data. Ever. |

## **1.2 The Founding Story**

| Built by the audience it serves Move was conceived by a 24-year-old who felt the problem firsthand: a city full of things to do and friends who want to do them, paralyzed by the coordination overhead. Two co-founders bring the operational and business depth to build it properly. The result is a product with genuine Gen Z instinct and Millennial execution \-- and a founding narrative that press, partners, and users will find immediately compelling. |
| :---- |

## **1.3 The Competitive Moat**

Move's long-term defensibility is not a feature list. It is architectural. The app is built with end-to-end encryption across location, communication, and social data \-- a genuine privacy moat that is difficult and expensive for competitors to replicate after the fact.

| Short-term moat | Frictionless AI-driven social coordination that no current app offers end-to-end. |

# **4\. Pricing & Monetization**

Move is free for all users at launch. The monetization model is built entirely around venues \-- structured to be as frictionless for venue partners as it is for users. No upfront costs, no monthly fees, no risk. Venues only pay when Move demonstrably delivers real customers through their door.

## **4.1 User Pricing: Free**

| The Logic Charging users at launch without a proven retention loop is a tax on growth. Move needs density in NYC to work well \-- the more friend groups using it, the stronger the AI gets and the more powerful the word-of-mouth. Free is not a concession. It is the growth strategy. Premium user features will be explored post product-market fit confirmation. |
| :---- |

## **4.2 Venue Revenue Model: $1 Per Verified Visit**

Venues opt into the Move partner network for free. They are only charged when a Move user physically visits their location and checks in through the app. The charge is $1 per verified person through the door.

| Venue cost to join | Zero. Free opt-in, no commitment, no monthly fee. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Charge trigger** | $1 per verified user check-in at that venue during an active Move. |
| **Billing cycle** | TBD \-- to be confirmed by the team (see Open Items). |
| **Payment collection** | TBD \-- to be confirmed by the team (see Open Items). |
| **Venue discount** | Venues offer a discount to Move users as an incentive to check in. Discount range is suggested by Move but set by the venue. Move takes no margin on the discount \-- it is a direct venue-to-user arrangement. |

## **4.3 Check-In Mechanics: Two Options**

Move supports two check-in paths. Both result in the same $1 per person charge to the venue.

| Method | How It Works | Best For | Friction Level |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Option A: Group Leader QR | One designated group member scans the venue's QR code on behalf of the whole party. Move logs all active party members and bills the venue per head. | Casual outings, bars, quick stops where individual scanning is impractical. | Very low |
| Option B: Individual Member QR | Each group member receives their own QR code to scan at the register. Scanning unlocks their pre-negotiated discount. Move bills the venue $1 per scan. | Restaurants, coffee shops, studios \-- anywhere a per-person transaction is natural. | Low to medium |

## **4.4 Making It Frictionless for Venues**

| At launch \-- manual QR | Move generates a static QR code for each venue. Staff see the customer's Move confirmation screen and apply the discount as they would any promo. No POS integration required. Works with any existing system from Day 1\. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Mid-term \-- promo code** | When a user checks in, the app generates a one-time alphanumeric code. The customer reads it to staff, who enter it into the POS as a discount line item. Still no integration required. |
| **Long-term \-- POS integration** | Direct integrations with Toast, Square, Clover, and Lightspeed. Discount triggers automatically at scan. Engineering investment required; targeted for Month 3-6 build. |
| **Staff briefing kit** | Move provides every partner venue a one-page back-of-house card explaining what Move customers will ask for and what the staff member does. Readable in 60 seconds. Eliminates transaction friction. |

## **4.5 Why This Model Works**

| The Pitch to Any Venue in 30 Seconds You pay nothing to join. You pay nothing until a Move user walks through your door. When they do, you pay one dollar. We tell you exactly how many people came in through Move and when. If it stops working for you, you opt out. There is no contract. |

# **7\. Retention & The Product Loop**

Acquisition is irrelevant without retention. The product loop is the marketing strategy. If users have a great first Move and come back, everything else compounds. If they do not, no amount of spending fixes it.

## **7.1 The Core Loop**

| The Loop User opens Move with friends \--\> Sets parameters \--\> Gets an AI plan \--\> Goes out \--\> Gets a venue discount \--\> Has a great time \--\> Saves the memory \--\> Invites new people next time. Every step must be fast, frictionless, and rewarding. Any friction is a leak. |
| :---- |

## **7.2 Retention Mechanics**

| Move history | Every outing saved as a memory. Users revisit where they went and who they were with. Creates emotional attachment. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Streak logic** | Consecutive weeks of Moves unlock exclusive NYC experiences surfaced only for active users. |
| **Friend group personalization** | The more a group uses Move, the better the AI learns their vibe. Personalization improves with use \-- a genuine retention incentive. |
| **City seasons** | Monthly thematic outing drops tied to NYC events and neighborhoods keep recommendations fresh. |
| **Venue discount habit** | Users associate going out through Move with getting a better deal. A retention loop that costs Move nothing. |

## **7.3 Retention Benchmarks**

| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |

# **11\. Key Risks & Mitigations**

| App not ready May 1 | Pre-launch activity is designed to be independent of the product. If May 1 slips, the waitlist and venue pipeline do not evaporate \-- they wait. Better to delay than to launch broken. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Cold start / density problem** | Launch in 2-3 NYC neighborhoods only. Density in a small area makes the social loop work. Do not go wide before going deep. |
| **AI plan quality is poor** | No public launch until beta testing (internal, team-only) confirms 70%+ plan quality satisfaction. Quality gates before scale. |
| **Venue check-in friction** | Option A group leader QR is the fallback for any venue where individual scanning creates service delays. Staff briefing kit is mandatory for every partner. |
| **Retention drops after Week 1** | Move history, streak logic, and city seasons are the retention levers. If D7 drops below 30%, activate one before spending another dollar on acquisition. |
| **Privacy architecture compromised** | No exceptions. No data selling. No third-party trackers. Any compromise permanently kills the Apple narrative and user trust. |
| **Funding pressure forces premature monetization** | Bootstrapped discipline means monetization activates after PMF \-- not before. Premature revenue at the cost of adoption is the wrong trade at this stage. |

# **13\. Glossary of Terms**

Quick reference for terminology used throughout this document. Intended for team members who may be less familiar with standard app growth and product marketing language.

| Term | Definition |
| :---- | :---- |
| **CAC** | Customer Acquisition Cost. The total cost of acquiring one new user, including any marketing, advertising, or referral spend divided by the number of new users acquired in that period. |
| **Churn** | The rate at which users stop using the app over a given period. High churn means people are downloading Move but not coming back. Low churn means the product is sticky. |
| **CPI** | Cost Per Install. In paid advertising, the average dollar cost to generate one app download. A CPI above $4 in Move's early stage is a signal to pause spend and reassess creative or targeting. |
| **D1 / D3 / D7 / D30 Retention** | The percentage of users who return to the app on Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, or Day 30 after their first download. These are the core health metrics for any consumer app. If 100 people download Move on Monday and 40 open it the following Monday, D7 retention is 40%. |
| **D30 Retention** | Specifically, the percentage of users still active 30 days after their first download. The most important long-term health signal for Move. A D30 above 25% at scale is considered strong for a social app. |
| **GTM** | Go-to-Market. The strategy and plan for how a product reaches its target users \-- covering positioning, pricing, launch timing, and acquisition channels. |
| **ICP** | Ideal Customer Profile. The specific description of the user Move is built for and most likely to retain. Move's ICP at launch is a 21-35 year old NYC-based social coordinator who is digitally native and frustrated by the coordination tax of going out. |
| **LTV** | Lifetime Value. The total revenue or value a single user generates over the entire time they use the product. For Move, LTV is currently driven by venue check-ins rather than direct user payments. |
| **MAU** | Monthly Active Users. The number of unique users who open and engage with Move at least once in a given calendar month. The primary scale metric for Move and the number that drives valuation conversations. |
| **Moat** | A competitive advantage that is difficult or expensive for competitors to replicate. Move's moat is its privacy-first encrypted architecture, which cannot be easily retrofitted by existing social apps. |
| **NPS** | Net Promoter Score. A measure of how likely users are to recommend Move to a friend, scored 0-100. An NPS above 50 is considered excellent. It is calculated by asking users "How likely are you to recommend Move?" and subtracting detractors from promoters. |
| **Organic social** | Content posted on TikTok, Instagram, and other platforms without paid promotion. Organic reach is earned through engagement quality rather than budget. Move's primary early acquisition channel. |
| **PMF** | Product-Market Fit. The point at which a product is meeting a real market need strongly enough that users actively seek it out, retain, and refer others. PMF is confirmed when retention benchmarks are consistently hit over multiple months. |
| **POS** | Point of Sale. The system venues use to process transactions \-- Toast, Square, Clover, and Lightspeed are the most common in NYC. Move's discount mechanic needs to work within or alongside these systems. |
| **PR / Earned Media** | Press coverage generated through media outreach rather than paid advertising. Gothamist, Time Out NY, The Verge, and similar outlets are earned media targets for Move's launch. |
| **Retention** | The ability of the app to keep users coming back after their first experience. The single most important metric at Move's stage. Poor retention makes every other metric meaningless. |
| **Soft launch** | A limited, controlled release of the app to a small group of users before the full public launch. Used to validate the product loop and fix issues before exposing the product to a mass audience. |
| **Valuation** | The estimated financial worth of the company, typically expressed as a multiple of revenue, MAU, or projected growth. Move's goal is to build a valuation large enough to support a significant acquisition or IPO exit. |
| **Waitlist** | A pre-launch sign-up list of users interested in early access to Move. Used to build a primed audience before the app is available and to generate referral-driven organic growth before spending on acquisition. |

| The North Star in One Sentence Move wins when a generation of people in cities around the world credits the app for some of the best nights of their lives \-- and when the company that made that possible is valued accordingly. |
