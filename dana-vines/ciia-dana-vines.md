# Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement

## The Move Social Inc.

---

**Effective Date:** _________________

This Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (the "CIIA") is entered into between:

- **The Move Social Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "Company")
- **Dana Vines**, an individual (the "Service Provider")

As a condition to the Service Provider's engagement with the Company and the issuance of restricted stock under the Company's Equity Incentive Plan, the Service Provider agrees to the following terms:

---

## 1. Confidential Information

### 1.1 Definition

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information relating to the Company's business, whether disclosed before or after the Effective Date, including but not limited to:

(a) **Technical information:** source code, algorithms, architectures, APIs, databases, system designs, encryption methods, security protocols, product roadmaps, and technical specifications;

(b) **Business information:** business plans, financial data, projections, pricing, revenue models, customer lists, vendor relationships, partnership terms, go-to-market strategies, and marketing plans;

(c) **User and market data:** user metrics, engagement data, growth data, market research, competitive analysis, and user feedback;

(d) **Operational information:** internal processes, employee and contractor compensation, organizational structure, and operational playbooks;

(e) **Legal and corporate information:** agreements, cap table details, fundraising plans, investor communications, and board materials;

(f) Any information designated as confidential by the Company, whether orally, in writing, or by the circumstances of disclosure.

### 1.2 Exceptions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Service Provider;

(b) Was known to the Service Provider prior to disclosure by the Company, as documented by written records predating the disclosure;

(c) Is independently developed by the Service Provider without use of or reference to Confidential Information, as documented by contemporaneous written records;

(d) Is disclosed to the Service Provider by a third party who has a legal right to make such disclosure without restriction.

### 1.3 Obligations

The Service Provider agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the Company's prior written consent;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing services for the Company;

(d) Take reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including but not limited to using password-protected devices, encrypted communications, and secure storage;

(e) Not remove Confidential Information from Company systems or premises without authorization.

### 1.4 Required Disclosures

If the Service Provider is required by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose Confidential Information, the Service Provider shall (a) promptly notify the Company in writing before making such disclosure (to the extent legally permitted), (b) cooperate with the Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and (c) disclose only the minimum amount of information required.

### 1.5 Duration

The obligations under this Section 1 shall survive termination of the Service Provider's relationship with the Company and remain in effect for five (5) years following such termination, except that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for as long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

---

## 2. Inventions and Intellectual Property

### 2.1 Prior Inventions

The Service Provider represents that no inventions, original works of authorship, developments, improvements, or trade secrets (collectively, "Prior Inventions") that (a) were made by the Service Provider prior to the Effective Date, (b) belong to the Service Provider, and (c) relate to the Company's current or anticipated business, exist as of the Effective Date. If any Prior Inventions do exist, the Service Provider shall disclose them in writing to the Company prior to executing this CIIA.

### 2.2 Assignment of Inventions

The Service Provider hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to any and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, works of authorship, designs, algorithms, source code, databases, trade secrets, and other intellectual property (collectively, "Inventions") that the Service Provider, solely or jointly with others, conceives, develops, or reduces to practice during the period of the Service Provider's engagement with the Company, if such Inventions:

(a) Are made using the Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Confidential Information;

(b) Result from any work performed by the Service Provider for the Company; or

(c) Relate to the Company's business or actual or demonstrably anticipated research or development.

### 2.3 Works Made for Hire

To the extent that any Invention constitutes a "work made for hire" under the United States Copyright Act, the Service Provider agrees that such work is a work made for hire and that the Company is the author thereof. To the extent any Invention does not qualify as a work made for hire, the Service Provider hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Invention, including all copyrights therein.

### 2.4 Moral Rights

To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the Service Provider waives any moral rights the Service Provider may have in any Inventions assigned to the Company under this CIIA, including rights of attribution, integrity, disclosure, and withdrawal.

### 2.5 Further Assurances

The Service Provider agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, evidence, or vest in the Company the rights assigned under this Section 2, including but not limited to patent applications, copyright registrations, and assignments. If the Service Provider is unable or unwilling to execute such documents, the Service Provider hereby irrevocably appoints the Company as the Service Provider's attorney-in-fact for such purpose, which appointment is coupled with an interest.

### 2.6 Records

The Service Provider agrees to keep and maintain adequate written records of all Inventions made during the engagement, in the form of notes, sketches, drawings, source code, or any other format that may be specified by the Company. Such records are the sole property of the Company.

---

## 3. Non-Solicitation

### 3.1 Employees and Contractors

During the Service Provider's engagement with the Company and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination of the engagement for any reason, the Service Provider shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or induce any employee, contractor, or advisor of the Company to terminate their relationship with the Company.

### 3.2 Clients and Partners

During the Service Provider's engagement with the Company and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination of the engagement for any reason, the Service Provider shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or divert any client, customer, or business partner of the Company with whom the Service Provider had material contact during the engagement, for the purpose of providing products or services competitive with the Company's business.

### 3.3 Reasonableness

The Service Provider acknowledges that the restrictions in this Section 3 are reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic area, and are necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests. If a court of competent jurisdiction determines that any restriction is overly broad, the parties agree that the court shall modify (rather than void) the restriction to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable.

---

## 4. Return of Materials

Upon termination of the Service Provider's engagement with the Company, or at any time upon the Company's request, the Service Provider shall promptly:

(a) Return to the Company all documents, files, materials, equipment, and other property belonging to the Company;

(b) Delete all Confidential Information from the Service Provider's personal devices, accounts, and storage;

(c) Provide written certification to the Company that all Company materials have been returned or destroyed.

---

## 5. Third-Party Information

The Service Provider agrees not to:

(a) Bring to the Company or use in the performance of services any confidential information or trade secrets belonging to any former employer or other third party;

(b) Violate any obligation of confidentiality owed to any third party in connection with services performed for the Company.

---

## 6. Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016 (18 U.S.C. § 1833(b)), the Service Provider is hereby notified that:

(a) An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made (i) in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law, or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

(b) An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, if the individual files any document containing the trade secret under seal and does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court order.

---

## 7. Dispute Resolution

### 7.1 Mediation

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this CIIA, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall first be submitted to mandatory mediation. The parties shall select a mutually agreeable mediator within ten (10) days of either party's written request for mediation. The mediation shall be completed within thirty (30) days of the mediator's appointment.

### 7.2 Binding Arbitration

If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in the State of Delaware by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA rules. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### 7.3 Arbitration Costs

The Company shall bear all arbitration fees, arbitrator compensation, and administrative costs that exceed the amount the Service Provider would have paid to file an equivalent action in state court in the State of Delaware. Each party shall bear its own attorneys' fees unless the arbitrator determines that one party's claims or defenses were frivolous, in which case the arbitrator may award reasonable attorneys' fees to the prevailing party.

### 7.4 Injunctive Relief

Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending the outcome of arbitration, including but not limited to enforcement of the confidentiality, non-solicitation, and intellectual property provisions of this CIIA.

---

## 8. General Provisions

### 8.1 Governing Law

This CIIA shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

### 8.2 Severability

If any provision of this CIIA is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### 8.3 Entire Agreement

This CIIA, together with the Independent Contractor Agreement, the Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement, and the Equity Incentive Plan between the Service Provider and the Company, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof. This CIIA supersedes Section 5 (Confidentiality and Intellectual Property) of the Independent Contractor Agreement to the extent of any conflict.

### 8.4 Amendment

This CIIA may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.

### 8.5 Survival

The obligations under Sections 1 (Confidential Information), 2 (Inventions), 3 (Non-Solicitation), 4 (Return of Materials), and 7 (Dispute Resolution) shall survive termination of the Service Provider's engagement with the Company.
