# Unanimous Written Consent of the Board of Directors

## The Move Social Inc.

*In Lieu of a Special Meeting*

---

**Date:** March 31, 2026

The undersigned, constituting all of the members of the Board of Directors of The Move Social Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "Corporation"), hereby adopt the following resolutions by unanimous written consent in lieu of a special meeting, pursuant to Section 141(f) of the General Corporation Law of the State of Delaware and Article III of the Corporation's Bylaws:

---

## Background

**WHEREAS**, on March 27, 2026, the Board authorized a restricted stock grant of 400,000 shares of Class B Common Stock to Dana Vines under the Corporation's Equity Incentive Plan (Resolution 8 of the First Meeting of the Board of Directors);

**WHEREAS**, the Board has reviewed the final form of the Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement (the "RSPA") and desires to ratify certain terms that differ from the general authorization in Resolution 8;

**WHEREAS**, the Board has determined that the Corporation should require a Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (the "CIIA") as a condition of Dana Vines' engagement and stock issuance;

---

## Resolution 1 — Ratification of Updated RSPA Terms

**RESOLVED**, that the Board hereby ratifies and approves the Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement between the Corporation and Dana Vines in its final form, including the following terms that amend or clarify the general authorization in Resolution 8 of the March 27, 2026 Board Meeting:

(a) **Unvested share forfeiture only.** Upon termination for Cause, only Unvested Shares are forfeited. Vested Shares are retained by the Purchaser, subject to transfer restrictions. This replaces the provision in Resolution 8 stating "immediate forfeiture of all shares upon termination for Cause."

(b) **Automatic forfeiture.** Unvested Shares are automatically forfeited upon termination of service (for any reason), without requiring the Company to exercise a repurchase right within a specified window. The Company shall pay the aggregate repurchase price ($0.00001 per unvested share) within thirty (30) days of termination.

(c) **Single-trigger acceleration on Change of Control.** Unless Unvested Shares are assumed or substituted by the acquiring entity, 100% of Unvested Shares automatically vest immediately prior to closing. If Unvested Shares are assumed or substituted, double-trigger acceleration applies upon involuntary termination or resignation for Good Reason within 12 months post-closing.

(d) **Floor Payout / Pari Passu clarification.** In any Change of Control, the Purchaser receives the greater of (i) the per-share Floor Payout under the Founders' Agreement carve-out and (ii) the per-share pari passu consideration payable to Class A stockholders.

(e) **Fractional share rounding.** Monthly vesting of 1/36th of remaining shares after the cliff, with fractional shares accumulating and vesting in full on the final vesting date.

(f) **Definition of Service.** "Service" includes engagement as employee, officer, director, consultant, or advisor. Capacity changes do not reset vesting, provided no gap exceeds 30 days.

(g) **Stock legend.** All certificates or book entries shall bear a restrictive legend noting vesting conditions, forfeiture provisions, transfer restrictions, and securities law restrictions.

(h) **Plan override for Change of Control.** The RSPA's preamble is amended so that Section 7 (Change of Control) of the RSPA controls over Section 8.2 of the Plan. This ensures the Administrator cannot cancel or alter Dana Vines' Change of Control protections (single-trigger and double-trigger acceleration, floor payout, pari passu treatment) by invoking Plan discretion under Section 8.2.

---

## Resolution 2 — Authorization of CIIA

**RESOLVED**, that the Board hereby authorizes and directs the officers to require Dana Vines to execute the Corporation's Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (CIIA) as a condition of her engagement and stock issuance.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the CIIA shall include provisions for: (a) confidentiality obligations surviving five (5) years post-termination (trade secrets surviving indefinitely); (b) assignment of all inventions and intellectual property created during the engagement; (c) non-solicitation of employees, contractors, and clients for twelve (12) months post-termination; (d) disclosure of prior inventions; and (e) return of all Company materials upon termination.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that the execution of the CIIA is a condition precedent to the issuance of Shares under the RSPA. No Shares shall be issued until the CIIA has been fully executed.

---

## Resolution 3 — Authorization of Contractor Agreement

**RESOLVED**, that the Board hereby ratifies the Independent Contractor Agreement between the Corporation and Dana Vines, which establishes her engagement as VP of Sales on an equity-only basis as an independent contractor.

**FURTHER RESOLVED**, that to the extent Section 5 (Confidentiality and Intellectual Property) of the Independent Contractor Agreement conflicts with the CIIA, the CIIA shall govern.

---

## Resolution 4 — Execution and Signing Order

**RESOLVED**, that the officers are authorized and directed to execute the following documents with Dana Vines, in the following order:

| Order | Document | Condition |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Confidential Information and Inventions Assignment Agreement (CIIA) | Must be signed before any other agreement |
| 2 | Independent Contractor Agreement | References CIIA and RSPA |

| 4 | Section 83(b) Election (Exhibit B of RSPA) | Must be filed within 30 days of Purchase Date |

---

## Resolution 5 — 83(b) Election Backstop

**RESOLVED**, that the officers shall implement the following backstop to ensure timely filing of Dana Vines' Section 83(b) election:

(a) Deliver a pre-filled 83(b) election form (Exhibit B of the RSPA) at the time of signing;

(b) Set a calendar reminder for twenty (20) days after the Purchase Date to confirm filing status;

(c) Provide written filing instructions, including the IRS Service Center address and certified mail procedure;

(d) Request proof of filing (filed form + certified mail receipt) within thirty-five (35) days of the Purchase Date;

(e) Note in the Corporation's records whether or not the election was timely filed.
