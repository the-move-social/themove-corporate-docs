## 2. Vesting Schedule

The option shall vest and become exercisable according to the following schedule:

| Milestone | Shares Vested | Cumulative |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| 1st anniversary of Vesting Commencement Date | 25% of total shares (25,000) | 25% |
| Each month thereafter for 36 months | 1/48 of total shares (~2,083) per month | 25% → 100% |

**Cliff:** No shares vest before the first anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date. If the Optionee's service terminates before the first anniversary, no shares vest and the option is forfeited in its entirety.

**Vesting requires continued service.** Vesting ceases immediately upon termination of the Optionee's service relationship with the Company.

## 3. Exercise of Option

### 3.1 Method of Exercise

The Optionee may exercise vested portions of the option by delivering to the Company:

(a) Written notice specifying the number of shares to be purchased; and

(b) Payment of the aggregate exercise price.

### 3.2 Payment Methods

The exercise price may be paid by:

(a) Cash or check payable to the Company;

(b) Such other methods as the Company may approve in writing.

### 3.3 Partial Exercise

The option may be exercised in whole or in part, provided that no partial exercise may be for fewer than 100 shares (or the remaining vested shares, if fewer than 100).

### 3.4 Early Exercise

Notwithstanding Section 3.1, the Optionee may exercise this option in whole or in part at any time after the Grant Date, including with respect to shares that have not yet vested ("Unvested Shares"). Any Unvested Shares acquired upon early exercise shall be subject to the Company's repurchase right described in Section 3.5. The Optionee acknowledges that early exercise of an NSO is a taxable event and that the Optionee should consult a tax advisor regarding the filing of an election under Section 83(b) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### 3.5 Company Repurchase Right

If the Optionee exercises this option with respect to Unvested Shares, such shares shall be subject to the Company's right to repurchase upon termination of the Optionee's service relationship with the Company. Upon termination for any reason other than Cause:

(a) The Company shall have the right, but not the obligation, to repurchase any Unvested Shares at the original exercise price paid per share;

(b) The Company must exercise this repurchase right within sixty (60) days following the date of termination;

(c) If the Company does not exercise the repurchase right within such period, the repurchase right shall lapse and the shares shall be free of such restriction.

Upon termination for Cause, all shares (vested and unvested) are forfeited pursuant to Section 4.2.

### 3.6 Section 83(b) Election Backstop

The Company shall provide the Optionee with the following upon early exercise:

(a) A pre-filled Section 83(b) election form;

(b) A calendar reminder twenty (20) days before the thirty (30) day filing deadline;

(c) Written instructions for filing the 83(b) election with the Internal Revenue Service via certified mail.

The Optionee shall deliver to the Company a copy of the filed 83(b) election and the certified mail receipt within thirty-five (35) days of exercise. Failure to provide proof of filing shall not affect the validity of the option grant but shall be noted in the Company's records.

---

## 4. Termination of Service

### 4.1 Voluntary Resignation or Termination Without Cause

If the Optionee's service terminates for any reason other than Cause, death, or disability:

(a) If the Optionee has **not** early exercised, the Optionee may exercise the vested portion of the option within **ninety (90) days** after termination. Any unvested portion is forfeited immediately.

(b) If the Optionee **has** early exercised, vested shares are retained by the Optionee. Unvested shares are subject to the Company's repurchase right under Section 3.5.

### 4.2 Termination for Cause

If the Optionee's service is terminated for cause, the entire option (vested and unvested) terminates immediately upon such termination.

### 4.3 Death or Disability

If the Optionee's service terminates due to death or disability, the Optionee (or the Optionee's estate) may exercise the vested portion of the option within **twelve (12) months** after the date of death or disability.

### 4.4 Expiration

In no event may the option be exercised after the Expiration Date specified in Section 1.

### 4.5 Definition of Cause

For purposes of this Agreement, "Cause" means (a) conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, a felony or any crime involving fraud, dishonesty, or moral turpitude; (b) willful misconduct or gross negligence in the performance of duties; (c) material breach of this Agreement or any other agreement with the Company that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice; or (d) willful failure to perform duties after written notice and a reasonable cure period.

---

## 5. Restrictions on Transfer

### 5.1 Non-Transferability

This option may not be sold, transferred, pledged, assigned, or otherwise alienated, except by will or the laws of descent and distribution. During the Optionee's lifetime, the option may be exercised only by the Optionee.

### 5.2 Shares Acquired on Exercise

Shares acquired upon exercise of the option shall be shares of **Class B Common Stock** (non-voting) and shall be subject to such transfer restrictions as the Company may impose, including but not limited to a right of first refusal in favor of the Company.

### 5.3 Non-Voting Shares

The Optionee acknowledges that all shares issuable upon exercise of this option are Class B Common Stock, which carries no voting rights. The Optionee shall have no right to vote on any matter submitted to stockholders of the Company, except as may be required by applicable law. Class B Common Stock has identical economic rights (dividends, distributions, liquidation proceeds) to Class A Common Stock on a per-share basis.

---

## 6. Change of Control

A "Change of Control" means (a) the sale of all or substantially all of the assets of the Company; (b) a merger, consolidation, or other business combination in which the stockholders of the Company immediately prior to such transaction hold less than 50% of the voting power of the surviving entity; or (c) a transaction or series of related transactions in which any person or group acquires more than 50% of the outstanding voting stock of the Company.

### 6.1 Floor Payout

In any Change of Control, the Optionee shall receive, at minimum, a cash payment equal to the positive difference (if any) between (i) the per-share consideration paid to stockholders in the transaction and (ii) the Exercise Price, multiplied by the number of vested shares subject to this option (the "Floor Payment"). The Floor Payment shall be made at the closing of the Change of Control transaction and shall not be subject to or reduced by any liquidation preference, participation right, or other priority payment owed to holders of preferred stock or any other class of securities. This provision is subject to and consistent with the acquisition carve-out set forth in Section 2.8 of the Founders' Agreement, which provides that no less than ten percent (10%) of the aggregate consideration payable to stockholders in any Change of Control transaction shall be allocated to holders of vested Class B Common Stock (and vested options exercisable for Class B Common Stock) on a pro rata basis, prior to the application of any liquidation preference.

### 6.2 Pari Passu Treatment

Shares of Class B Common Stock issued or issuable upon exercise of this option shall be treated on a per-share basis identically to shares of Class A Common Stock in any Change of Control transaction. No liquidation preference or participation right shall reduce the per-share consideration payable to the Optionee below the per-share consideration payable to holders of Class A Common Stock.

### 6.3 Acceleration

If the Administrator does not take action under Section 8.2(a), (b), or (c) of the Plan within ten (10) days prior to the Change of Control, all unvested shares subject to this option shall automatically vest in full immediately prior to the closing of the transaction.

## 7. Tax Matters

### 7.1 Tax Responsibility

The Optionee is solely responsible for all tax consequences arising from the grant, vesting, and exercise of this option. The Company makes no representations regarding the tax treatment of this option.

### 7.2 NSO Tax Treatment

This option is a Non-Qualified Stock Option. The Optionee acknowledges that:

(a) Upon exercise, the Optionee will recognize ordinary income equal to the difference between the fair market value of the shares on the date of exercise and the exercise price;

(b) If the Optionee early exercises and files a valid Section 83(b) election within thirty (30) days of exercise, the taxable spread is measured at the time of exercise (which may be near zero if exercised shortly after grant at the 409A fair market value);

(c) Subsequent appreciation above the value at exercise (or at the time of 83(b) election) will be taxed as capital gain upon sale, with long-term capital gains rates applying if the shares are held for more than one (1) year from the date of exercise.

### 7.3 Withholding

The Company shall have the right to withhold from any issuance of shares any amounts necessary to satisfy applicable tax withholding obligations.

## 12. Dispute Resolution

### 12.1 Mediation

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall first be submitted to mandatory mediation. The parties shall select a mutually agreeable mediator within ten (10) days of either party's written request for mediation. The mediation shall be completed within thirty (30) days of the mediator's appointment.

### 12.2 Binding Arbitration

If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in the State of Delaware by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA rules. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.
