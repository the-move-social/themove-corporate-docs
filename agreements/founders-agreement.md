## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 "Change of Control"

A "Change of Control" means (a) the sale of all or substantially all of the assets of the Company; (b) a merger, consolidation, or other business combination in which the stockholders of the Company immediately prior to such transaction hold less than 50% of the voting power of the surviving entity; or (c) a transaction or series of related transactions in which any person or group acquires more than 50% of the outstanding voting stock of the Company.

### 1.2 "Cause"

"Cause" means (a) conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, a felony or any crime involving fraud, dishonesty, or moral turpitude; (b) willful misconduct or gross negligence in the performance of duties; (c) material breach of this Agreement or any other agreement with the Company that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice; or (d) willful failure to perform duties after written notice and a reasonable cure period.

### 1.3 "Fair Market Value" or "FMV"

"Fair Market Value" means the value per share as determined by a qualified independent appraiser mutually agreed upon by the Founders (or, if they cannot agree, each Founder shall select one appraiser and those two appraisers shall select a third, whose determination shall be binding). The cost of the appraisal shall be borne by the Company.

### 1.4 "Board"

"Board" means the Board of Directors of PTL, unless otherwise specified.

### 1.5 Incapacity and Disability

(a) **Incapacity (15+ consecutive days).** If a Founder is unable to perform the essential functions of their role, with or without reasonable accommodation, for fifteen (15) or more consecutive days due to physical or mental illness, injury, or impairment, the incapacity proxy designated by such Founder pursuant to Section 1.5(d) may act on the incapacitated Founder's behalf for routine operational matters, including: authorizing expenditures within existing spending caps, performance of existing contracts, and payment of recurring obligations. The incapacity proxy shall not have authority to bind the Company to new material obligations or to vote on Board matters beyond routine operations.

(b) **Extended Incapacity (90+ consecutive days).** If a Founder's incapacity exceeds ninety (90) consecutive days, the remaining director and the independent tiebreaker designated by the incapacitated Founder pursuant to Section 1.5(d) shall together constitute a quorum of the Board for all purposes and may take any actions reasonably necessary for the continued operation of the Company and TMS.

(c) **Disability (180+ consecutive days).** If a Founder's incapacity exceeds one hundred eighty (180) consecutive days ("Disability"), the remaining Founder shall have the right (but not the obligation) to purchase the Disabled Founder's shares at Fair Market Value, determined by an independent appraiser in accordance with Section 1.3. The remaining Founder shall deliver written notice of election to purchase within thirty (30) days of the Disability determination, and the closing shall occur within sixty (60) days thereafter. If the remaining Founder does not elect to purchase, the Disabled Founder (or their representative) shall retain their shares, subject to the other provisions of this Agreement.

(d) **Designations.** Within thirty (30) days of the execution of this Agreement, each Founder shall designate in writing: (i) an incapacity proxy who may act on such Founder's behalf in the event of incapacity under Section 1.5(a); and (ii) an independent tiebreaker who shall serve alongside the remaining director in the event of extended incapacity under Section 1.5(b). Each Founder may update these designations at any time by written notice to the other Founder and to the Company. The designations shall be maintained in the Company's corporate records.

## 2. Equity and Vesting

### 2.1 Initial Equity

Each Founder holds 5,000,000 shares of Common Stock of PTL, par value $0.00001 per share, representing 50% of the total authorized shares.

### 2.2 Vesting Schedule

Each Founder's shares shall be subject to the following vesting schedule:

(a) **Vesting Period:** Four (4) years from the date of this Agreement (the "Vesting Commencement Date").

(b) **Cliff:** No shares shall vest during the first twelve (12) months following the Vesting Commencement Date (the "Cliff Period"). On the first anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date, 25% (1,250,000 shares) shall vest.

(c) **Monthly Vesting:** Following the Cliff Period, the remaining 75% (3,750,000 shares) shall vest in equal monthly installments over the remaining thirty-six (36) months, at a rate of 104,166 shares per month (with the final month adjusted for rounding).

### 2.3 Company Repurchase Right

The Company shall have the right, but not the obligation, to repurchase any unvested shares upon a Founder's termination of service for any reason, at the original cost of $0.00001 per share. This repurchase right shall be exercised by written notice within ninety (90) days of termination.

### 2.4 Cessation of Vesting

Vesting shall immediately cease upon a Founder's termination of service to the Company, whether by resignation, removal, death, or Disability.

### 2.5 Acceleration on Change of Control

(a) **Mutual Acceleration.** One hundred percent (100%) of each Founder's unvested shares shall immediately vest upon a Change of Control if both Founders consent in writing to such acceleration.

(b) **Automatic Acceleration.** One hundred percent (100%) of each Founder's unvested shares shall immediately vest upon a Change of Control if the acquiring entity does not offer such Founder a role with substantially equivalent responsibilities, authority, and compensation to the role held immediately prior to the Change of Control.

## 3. Buy-Sell (Shotgun) Provisions

### 3.1 Preconditions

The buy-sell mechanism described in this Section 3 may only be invoked after the following conditions have been met:

(a) A deadlock has occurred at the TMS Board level or the PTL Board level on a specific matter;

(b) The deadlocked matter has been submitted to mediation pursuant to Section 6.2 and mediation has been unsuccessful within thirty (30) days;

(c) If the deadlock originated at the TMS Board, it shall be treated as a deadlock at both the TMS and PTL Board levels for purposes of this Section 3.

### 3.2 Shotgun Mechanism

Upon satisfaction of the preconditions in Section 3.1, either Founder (the "Offering Founder") may initiate the buy-sell process by delivering a written offer (the "Shotgun Notice") to the other Founder (the "Receiving Founder") specifying:

(a) The price per share at which the Offering Founder offers to purchase all of the Receiving Founder's shares of PTL Common Stock (the "Offer Price"); and

(b) The material terms of the proposed transaction.

### 3.3 Response

Within thirty (30) days of receipt of the Shotgun Notice, the Receiving Founder must elect one of the following by written notice to the Offering Founder:

(a) **Accept:** Sell all of the Receiving Founder's shares to the Offering Founder at the Offer Price; or

(b) **Reverse:** Purchase all of the Offering Founder's shares at the same Offer Price per share.

If the Receiving Founder fails to respond within the thirty (30) day period, the Receiving Founder shall be deemed to have elected to sell at the Offer Price.

### 3.4 Payment and Closing

(a) Payment shall be made in cash or Company-approved financing.

(b) Closing shall occur within sixty (60) days of the Receiving Founder's election (or deemed election) under Section 3.3.

(c) At closing, the selling Founder shall deliver all shares, duly endorsed for transfer, free and clear of all liens and encumbrances, and shall execute all documents reasonably necessary to effect the transfer.

### 3.5 Effect of Buy-Sell

Upon closing of the buy-sell transaction, the selling Founder shall resign from all positions as director, officer, and any other role with PTL and TMS, and the non-compete and non-solicitation provisions of Sections 4 and 5 shall apply.

## 4. Non-Compete

### 4.1 Restriction

During the term of each Founder's service to the Company and for a period of twelve (12) months following the date of such Founder's departure from the Company (whether by resignation, removal, buy-sell, or otherwise), such Founder shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, participate in, consult for, or be connected in any manner with any business that competes with the Company or TMS within the Restricted Scope and Restricted Geography.

### 4.2 Restricted Scope

The "Restricted Scope" means the development, operation, or marketing of: (a) in-real-life (IRL) social or adventure applications; and (b) social event platforms.

### 4.3 Restricted Geography

The "Restricted Geography" means the United States.

### 4.4 Reasonableness

Each Founder acknowledges that the restrictions set forth in this Section 4 are reasonable and narrowly tailored in scope, geography, and duration, and are necessary to protect the legitimate business interests of the Company.

---

## 5. Non-Solicitation

### 5.1 Restriction on Soliciting Personnel

During the term of each Founder's service to the Company and for a period of twelve (12) months following the date of such Founder's departure from the Company (whether by resignation, removal, buy-sell, or otherwise), such Founder shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, or engage any employee, contractor, or consultant of PTL or TMS, or encourage any such person to terminate their relationship with PTL or TMS.

### 5.2 Restriction on Soliciting Customers

During the same period described in Section 5.1, such Founder shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, divert, or take away any customer, user, or business relationship of PTL or TMS, or attempt to do so.

## 6. Dispute Resolution

### 6.1 Good Faith Negotiation

The Founders shall first attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement through good faith negotiation for a period of fifteen (15) days following written notice of the dispute by either Founder to the other.

### 6.2 Mediation

If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation within the fifteen (15) day period, the dispute shall be submitted to mediation. The Founders shall select a mutually agreeable mediator within ten (10) days. The mediation shall be completed within thirty (30) days of the mediator's appointment.

### 6.3 Binding Arbitration

If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in the State of Delaware by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA rules. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

---

## 7. Death

### 7.1 Company Repurchase Right

Upon the death of a Founder, the Company shall have the right (but not the obligation) to repurchase the deceased Founder's shares at Fair Market Value, determined by an independent appraiser in accordance with Section 1.3.

### 7.2 Estate Sale Right

The estate of the deceased Founder shall have one hundred eighty (180) days from the date of death to sell the deceased Founder's shares back to the Company or to the remaining Founder at Fair Market Value. If the estate does not exercise this right within the 180-day period, the shares shall remain subject to the other provisions of this Agreement.

### 7.3 Key-Person Life Insurance

The Company may maintain key-person life insurance policies on each Founder. Any proceeds received by the Company from such policies shall be applied toward the purchase price of the deceased Founder's shares under this Section 7.

---

## 8. Voluntary Departure

### 8.1 Notice Requirement

A Founder who voluntarily departs the Company shall provide ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other Founder and to the Company (the "Notice Period").

### 8.2 Unvested Shares

Upon voluntary departure, all unvested shares shall be subject to the Company's repurchase right at original cost ($0.00001 per share) as set forth in Section 2.3.

### 8.3 Vested Shares

Vested shares held by the departing Founder shall be subject to the right of first refusal ("ROFR") provisions set forth in the Stockholders' Agreement of PTL, if any, or as otherwise agreed by the Founders.

### 8.4 Surviving Obligations

The non-compete provisions of Section 4 and the non-solicitation provisions of Section 5 shall survive voluntary departure and remain in effect for the periods specified therein.

## 9. Confidentiality

### 9.1 Confidential Information

Each Founder acknowledges that during the course of service to the Company, such Founder will have access to and become acquainted with confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company and TMS, including but not limited to: business plans, financial data, customer and user data, technology, source code, algorithms, trade secrets, marketing strategies, and other proprietary information (collectively, "Confidential Information").

### 9.2 Non-Disclosure

Each Founder agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not to disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal any Confidential Information to any third party during the term of service or at any time thereafter, except as required in the performance of duties for the Company or as authorized in writing by the Board.

### 9.3 Survival

(a) **Trade Secrets.** The obligation of confidentiality with respect to trade secrets shall survive termination of this Agreement indefinitely, or for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

(b) **Other Confidential Information.** The obligation of confidentiality with respect to all other Confidential Information shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

## 10. Intellectual Property

### 10.1 Assignment

Each Founder agrees that all inventions, discoveries, developments, improvements, works of authorship, software, designs, and other intellectual property (collectively, "Work Product") conceived, created, or developed by such Founder during the term of service to the Company, whether or not during working hours, that relate to the Company's or TMS's actual or anticipated business, research, or development, shall be the sole and exclusive property of PTL. Each Founder hereby irrevocably assigns to PTL all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product.

### 10.2 Pre-Existing IP

Each Founder acknowledges that any pre-existing intellectual property contributed to or used in connection with the Company's business shall be governed by a separate IP Assignment Agreement between such Founder and PTL.

### 10.3 Cooperation

Each Founder agrees to cooperate fully with the Company in securing, maintaining, and enforcing the Company's rights in any Work Product, including executing any documents and taking any actions reasonably required to perfect such rights.
