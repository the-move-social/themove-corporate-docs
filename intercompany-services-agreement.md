# Intercompany Services Agreement

---

**Date:** _________, 2026

This Intercompany Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into between:

- **Present Tense Labs, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "Provider")
- **The Move Social, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "Recipient")

The Provider and the Recipient are under common control, with the same individuals (Altin Lecaj and Eric Garcia) serving as the officers and directors of both entities.

---

## Recitals

A. The Provider owns and maintains certain technology infrastructure, systems, and resources that are shared with its affiliated entities.

B. The Recipient desires to receive certain services from the Provider, and the Provider is willing to provide such services, on the terms set forth herein.

C. This Agreement is separate from and in addition to the Intellectual Property License Agreement between the parties dated _________, 2026.

---

## 1. Services

### 1.1 Shared Services

The Provider shall make the following services available to the Recipient (the "Services"):

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Infrastructure** | Cloud computing resources, hosting, server management, and deployment pipelines |
| **ML/AI Operations** | Training, deployment, and maintenance of shared machine learning models |
| **Data Infrastructure** | Vector databases, data pipelines, storage, and backup systems |
| **DevOps** | CI/CD pipelines, monitoring, alerting, and incident response infrastructure |
| **Security** | Security monitoring, vulnerability management, and compliance infrastructure |
| **Administrative** | Shared administrative services including bookkeeping, tax preparation coordination, and corporate compliance |

### 1.2 Service Modifications

The parties may modify the scope of Services by mutual written agreement. The Provider may add new services upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the Recipient. The Provider may discontinue existing services only upon one hundred eighty (180) days' written notice and only if the Recipient has been provided a reasonable transition plan. Critical Services (Infrastructure, Data Infrastructure, and Security) may not be discontinued while this Agreement is in effect except by mutual written agreement.

### 1.3 Service Standards

The Provider shall perform the Services with reasonable care and in a professional manner. The Provider does not guarantee uninterrupted availability of any Service.

---

## 2. Fees and Payment

### 2.1 Initial Fee

The initial fee for the Services is **$0.00 per month.** The parties acknowledge that during the early stage of both companies, shared infrastructure costs are minimal and a formal cost allocation is not yet necessary.

### 2.2 Future Cost Allocation

When shared infrastructure costs become material, the parties shall negotiate a fair cost allocation based on one of the following methods:

(a) **Direct allocation:** Costs directly attributable to the Recipient are charged to the Recipient;

(b) **Proportional allocation:** Shared costs are allocated based on the Recipient's proportional usage (e.g., compute usage, storage, API calls);

(c) **Fixed fee:** A monthly fixed fee covering all Services.

### 2.3 Arm's Length Requirement

Any fees charged under this Agreement must be at arm's length — consistent with what would be charged between unrelated parties for comparable services. The parties shall maintain documentation supporting the reasonableness of any fees.

### 2.4 Payment Terms

Fees, when established, shall be invoiced monthly and payable within thirty (30) days of invoice date.

### 2.5 Related-Party Transaction Review

The parties acknowledge that they share common directors and officers. Any change to the fee structure under this Agreement, and any new intercompany transaction between the parties, shall be reviewed by an independent accountant or advisor to confirm arm's-length pricing. The parties shall maintain written documentation of the arm's-length basis for all intercompany transactions, updated annually.

---

## 3. Data and Privacy

### 3.1 Data Ownership

All data processed by the Provider on behalf of the Recipient remains the property of the Recipient. The Provider shall not use the Recipient's data for any purpose other than providing the Services.

### 3.2 Data Protection

The Provider shall implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect the Recipient's data from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

### 3.3 Regulatory Compliance

Each party shall comply with all applicable data protection and privacy laws in connection with the Services.

---

## 4. Confidentiality

Each party shall maintain in confidence all confidential information of the other party received in connection with this Agreement. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

---

## 5. Limitation of Liability

### 5.1 No Consequential Damages

Neither party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages arising out of or related to this Agreement.

### 5.2 Maximum Liability

The Provider's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the greater of (a) the total fees paid by the Recipient to the Provider in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (b) $10,000.

---

## 6. Term and Termination

### 6.1 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the date first written above and shall continue until terminated in accordance with this Section.

### 6.2 Termination

This Agreement may be terminated:

(a) By mutual written agreement of the parties;

(b) By the Recipient upon ninety (90) days' written notice. The Provider may terminate this Agreement only upon one hundred eighty (180) days' written notice, and only if a reasonable transition plan has been provided to the Recipient. Notwithstanding the foregoing, so long as the IP License Agreement between the parties remains in effect, the Provider may not terminate this Agreement without the Recipient's written consent;

(c) Automatically upon the Provider and the Recipient ceasing to be under Common Control (as defined in the Intellectual Property License Agreement between the parties);

(d) Immediately by either party if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of written notice.

### 6.3 Transition

Upon termination, the Provider shall provide reasonable assistance to the Recipient in transitioning the Services to the Recipient's own infrastructure or to a third-party provider. Such transition assistance shall be provided for a period of up to ninety (90) days following termination.

---

## 7. Independent Contractors

The parties are independent entities. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the parties.

---

## 8. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

---

## 9. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the shared services described herein and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings on this subject.

---

## 10. Amendment

This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both parties.

---

## 11. Dispute Resolution

### 11.1 Mediation

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall first be submitted to mandatory mediation. The parties shall select a mutually agreeable mediator within fifteen (15) days of either party's written request for mediation. The mediation shall be completed within thirty (30) days of the mediator's appointment.

### 11.2 Binding Arbitration

If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in the State of Delaware by a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA rules. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### 11.3 Litigation

Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending the outcome of mediation or arbitration.
