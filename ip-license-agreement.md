If intellectual property could reasonably power a product outside the Field of Use, it is an Improvement to the Platform IP and is owned by the Licensor. If it is specific to the Licensee Products and has no reasonable application outside the Field of Use, it is Licensee-Owned IP.

In the event of a dispute over classification, the Board of Directors of the Licensor shall make the determination in good faith. If the Board cannot reach agreement within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be submitted to a mutually agreed independent technology valuation expert, whose determination shall be binding. If the parties cannot agree on an expert within fifteen (15) days, the expert shall be appointed by the American Arbitration Association.

## 5. Term and Termination

### 5.1 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the date first written above and shall continue in perpetuity, subject to termination as provided herein.

### 5.2 Termination Events

This Agreement shall terminate automatically upon the occurrence of any of the following:

(a) The Licensor and the Licensee cease to be under Common Control. "Common Control" means that the same individuals collectively hold a majority of the outstanding voting shares (Class A Common Stock) of both the Licensor and the Licensee. For purposes of this section, shares of Class B Common Stock (non-voting) issued under any equity incentive plan shall not be counted toward the determination of majority control;

(b) The Licensee is acquired by a third party (whether by merger, stock purchase, or asset purchase);

(c) The Licensee is dissolved or liquidated;

(d) By mutual written agreement of the parties.

### 5.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

(a) The Licensee shall immediately cease all use of the Platform IP;

(b) The Licensee shall return or destroy all copies of the Platform IP in its possession;

(c) The Licensee shall retain ownership of all Licensee-Owned IP;

(d) Any sublicenses granted with Licensor consent shall terminate simultaneously unless the Licensor agrees otherwise in writing.

Sections 3 (Ownership), 6 (Representations), 7 (Confidentiality), and 9 (Governing Law) shall survive termination.
