## Annual Filings

### Federal Corporate Income Tax — IRS Form 1120

- **Due:** April 15 (or next business day) for prior calendar year
- **Extension:** File Form 7004 for automatic 6-month extension (to October 15)
- **Applies to:** PTL and TMS (separate returns, or consolidated return if elected)
- **Filing:** PTL and TMS file separate federal returns. PTL does not own TMS stock (the founders hold TMS Class A shares directly), so consolidated filing is not available.
- **Key items to report:**
  - Stock option exercises and repurchases
  - Related-party transactions between PTL and TMS (common officers/directors)

### Delaware Franchise Tax

- **Due:** March 1 each year
- **Applies to:** PTL and TMS (each files separately with Delaware)
- **Method:** Authorized Shares method or Assumed Par Value Capital method — calculate both and use whichever is lower
- **Annual report:** Filed simultaneously with franchise tax payment via Delaware Division of Corporations

### New York State and City Tax

- **Due:** Follows federal due dates (April 15 / October 15 with extension)
- **Applies to:** PTL and TMS if operating from New York (foreign-qualified)
- **Forms:** NY Form CT-3 (General Business Corporation Franchise Tax Return) for each entity
- **NYC:** If operating from NYC, also file NYC Form NYC-4S or NYC-4S-EZ (General Corporation Tax)

## Quarterly Obligations

### Estimated Federal Tax Payments — IRS Form 1120-W

- **Due:** April 15, June 15, September 15, December 15
- **Applies to:** PTL and TMS if expecting to owe $500+ in tax for the year
- **Penalty:** Underpayment penalties apply if estimates are insufficient

### Estimated State/City Tax Payments

- **Due:** Follows quarterly schedule similar to federal
- **Applies to:** PTL and TMS if operating in New York

## Contractor Reporting

### IRS Form 1099-NEC

- **Due:** January 31 for prior calendar year (to contractors and IRS)
- **Applies to:** TMS for each independent contractor paid $600+ in non-employee compensation during the year
- **Current contractors:** Dana Vines (equity-only compensation — see note below)
- **Equity-only note:** NSO exercises are taxable events. When a contractor exercises options, the spread (FMV minus exercise price) is reportable as non-employee compensation on Form 1099-NEC. Track exercise dates and FMV at exercise.

## Stock Option & Equity Events

### 83(b) Elections (Individual Filers)

- **Filed by:** Each individual who early-exercises stock options
- **Deadline:** 30 days from exercise date (no extensions, no exceptions)
- **Company responsibility:** Collect proof of filing from each optionee within 35 days of exercise. Keep copies in corporate records.
- **Tax return:** Each filer attaches a copy to their personal tax return for the year of exercise

### NSO Exercise Reporting

- **When a contractor exercises NSOs:** The spread (FMV at exercise minus exercise price) is ordinary income to the contractor and reported on Form 1099-NEC
- **When a founder/officer exercises NSOs:** Same treatment since Altin and Eric are consultants to TMS (not employees). Report on Form 1099-NEC.
- **Company records:** Log every exercise in the stock ledger with date, number of shares, exercise price, and FMV at exercise

### Stock Repurchases

- **If the company repurchases unvested shares:** Record the repurchase in the stock ledger. The tax treatment depends on whether the individual filed an 83(b) election. Consult tax advisor for proper reporting.
